Two weeks of silence

Project Loffline

It's been over two weeks since I started Project Loffline. A nice milestone to share some of the highs and lows with you. If you are in the middle of it and are wondering what the hell this is about, click here to start at the beginning.

Getting up on time has been a lot easier for me in the last few weeks

Maybe I am going crazy and this has nothing to do with my experiment, but the craziest experience in the last two weeks was that one day I woke up... at 5:00 in the morning. I had gone to bed at 11:00 p.m. because I had a meeting at 9:30 a.m. When I was still awake at 5:45 a.m. I just got out of bed. I did some things that I would otherwise have had to do after the meeting. I wasn't tired for the rest of the day, until it was time to go to bed again. Getting up early has never been my thing and nothing like this had ever happened to me before. Getting up on time has been a lot easier for me in the last few weeks. Of course I would like to link this to my experiment. A possible explanation is that when I used my phone, I sometimes did this when I was doing something else simultaniously. While I was talking to someone, while I was cycling, while I was studying. I can imagine that constantly switching between the phone and another activity takes a lot of energy, energy that I often save now.

"I thought, I'll text him, but that slipped my mind"

So, what about communication. A week ago I had a party for someone's birthday. Just before I left home, I realized that I had nothing to give. I hadn't heard from anyone about a joint gift either. You guessed it, there was something organized, but this was discussed through a group chat. They forgot about me. "I thought, I'll text him, but that slipped my mind". That was the explanation that I got. Anyway, fortunately It was possible for me to make a contribution last minute. Despite the fact that I found this kind of group chats useful, I also like to see the positive side of this situation. In a previous blog I already described that the use of a phone according to my hypothesis can lead to a "lazy memory". I will probably have to take more initiative for these kinds of occasions now. I expect that in the long run this will lead to a better memory of the upcoming activities that I have and thus to better preparation for as well.

...about 10 messages back and forth could not lead to a really clear appointment

How do I communicate now? You might not guess it, but I have been using the sms again since two weeks! Super old-fashioned, but very effective. Most conversations consist of asking a question with an answer in return. Or, for example, to confirm an appointment. Up until now, things haven't always been going so well every time. The first sms conversation since I've been without Whatsapp, was in my opinion super awkward. I had to make a quick appointment with someone where we would meet each other while walking. That didn't work out very well. Ever-changing plans, also depending on some environmental factors, did not help at all and about 10 messages back and forth could not lead to a really clear appointment. I think it would have been a lot easier to make a phone call. I do think that this is a typical consequence of the continuous accessibility that we enjoy nowadays. Because we assume that we can always reach each other, we wait until the very last minute to make good arrangements.

In my search for the most practical and minimal use of my phone, I have perhaps also made some mistakes with my texting over the past few weeks. My messages were pretty formal and if I knew what I needed to know I wouldn't answer anymore. I got the feedback that a thank you would be nice. I will try to do better in the future!

Using your phone all the time doesn't really invite to start a conversation

It was two weeks of highs and lows. There were irritations. One afternoon when I was chilling with a friend, I thought he was checking his phone all the time. He happened to be chatting with a nice girl and I don't think he left a moment untouched to pay attention to that conversation. I noticed that I found this rather annoying. My phone was well tucked inside my pocket and where I tried to use it as little as possible, I felt like he was only using it more and more. Luckily I have the self-control of an old Zen master and I first checked with myself whether this could be my own fault, before I discussed this with my friend. Probably I am used to pick up my phone when someone else does, but now that I don't really have any reason to do so anymore, I'm bothered by it when someone else uses his phone a lot. I do find that using your phone doesn't really invite to start a conversation. So if you don't want to put my self-control to the test, just put your phone somewhere where the sun isn't shining when I'm around.

Suddenly I understand the usefulness that women see in a handbag

A very positive side effect of my experiment is that many people ask me how I like it without Whatsapp. So I almost always have a subject for discussion. In my first blog I also indicated that I will try to leave my phone at home at least once a week when I leave the house myself. I do and I really like it. There are several times in the week when I really don't need my phone. Even more often I leave my phone in my jacket when I'm visiting someone. The jacket is hanging on the coat rack and I don't have my phone with me. For me this is actually a simple trick not to grab my phone too quickly. I'm surprised that I've never thought of this before, because I don't like a phone in my pocket at all. I don't know how I'm going to solve this later in the summer when I don't have to wear a jacket anymore. Suddenly I understand the usefulness that women see in a handbag.

That remark from the phone where the sun isn't shining, that's a joke, of course. Forcing my ideals on someone else is the last thing I want. Although I do think for the fact that together is always better than alone. If, as a reader, you feel inspired by Project Loffline, I would like to invite you to join me. In doing so, I would like to emphasize once again the general idea: to return to the most practical use of smartphones. Your view on practical use may differ from mine. In other words, I do not expect the same measures as I am taking. So if you notice that you are grabbing your phone a little more often than you would like, please contact me. I would like to exchange ideas and think along with you about what you could do about it!
